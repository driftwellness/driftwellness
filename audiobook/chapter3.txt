Chapter 3: The Evening Wind-Down – Preparing for Rest

Welcome. As the day comes to a close, it's time to let go. To release the weight of everything you've carried, and to prepare your body and mind for rest.

This chapter is about creating a ritual—a gentle transition from the noise of the day to the stillness of the night.

Let's begin.

Find a comfortable place to sit or lie down. Somewhere quiet, where you won't be disturbed.

Take a deep breath in... and let it out with a sigh.

Good.

Now, I want you to think about your day. Not to analyze it, not to judge it, but simply to acknowledge it.

"This was my day. And now it's done."

So often, we carry the day with us into the night. We replay conversations, worry about tomorrow, or mentally write our to-do lists.

But tonight, we're going to practice letting go.

Let's start with a simple visualization.

Imagine that you're standing at the edge of the ocean. The sun is setting, painting the sky in shades of orange, pink, and gold.

The waves are gentle, rolling in and out with a soothing rhythm.

Now, imagine that each wave represents something from your day. A task you completed. A conversation you had. A worry you've been holding onto.

As each wave comes in, it brings that thought or feeling to the shore.

And as it rolls back out, it takes it with it. Out to sea. Away from you.

Let's try it together.

Breathe in... and as you breathe out, imagine a wave rolling in, carrying something from your day.

Maybe it's a stressful meeting. Maybe it's an email you forgot to send. Maybe it's just the general busyness of life.

Now, as you breathe in again, watch that wave roll back out, taking it with it.

Gone. Released. No longer yours to carry.

Let's do that again.

Breathe in... and out. Another wave. Another release.

In... and out.

In... and out.

Beautiful.

Now, as you continue to breathe, I want you to scan your body for any tension you might be holding.

Start at the top of your head. Is there tension in your forehead? Your jaw? Your neck?

Just notice it. Don't try to fix it. Just acknowledge it.

"Yes, I'm holding tension here."

Now, as you breathe out, imagine that tension melting away. Like ice melting in the sun.

Breathe in... and out. Let it go.

Move down to your shoulders. Are they tight? Raised? Holding the weight of the day?

Breathe in... and as you breathe out, let them drop. Heavy. Relaxed.

Good.

Now your chest. Your stomach. Your hips.

Breathe in... and out. Let the tension dissolve.

Your legs. Your feet. All the way down to your toes.

Breathe in... and out. Let it all go.

Perfect.

Now, I want you to set an intention for your evening. Not a task, but a feeling.

How do you want to feel as you drift off to sleep?

Maybe it's peaceful. Maybe it's grateful. Maybe it's simply... empty. In a good way. Like a clean slate.

Whatever it is, hold that word in your mind.

And as you breathe, imagine you're filling yourself with that feeling.

Breathe in peace. Breathe out tension.

Breathe in gratitude. Breathe out worry.

Breathe in stillness. Breathe out noise.

In... and out.

In... and out.

As we come to the end of this practice, I want you to take one final, deep breath.

Fill your lungs completely... hold it for just a moment... and then let it go with a long, slow exhale.

Ahhh.

Beautiful.

When you're ready, gently open your eyes. Or, if you're already in bed, simply let yourself drift.

You've done enough today. You've been enough today.

Now, it's time to rest.

Thank you for being here. Thank you for choosing yourself.

Take a deep breath in... and let the evening carry you into stillness.

Welcome to your pusterom. Welcome to Drift.
