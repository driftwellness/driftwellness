Chapter 1: The Morning Breath – Starting Your Day with Intention

Welcome. You've taken the first step toward creating space for yourself. A space to breathe, to be, and to simply exist without the weight of the world pressing down on you.

This is your pusterom—your breathing room.

In this first chapter, we're going to explore the power of the morning breath. Not as a task to check off your list, but as a gentle invitation to meet yourself exactly where you are.

So, find a comfortable place to sit or lie down. Let your body settle. There's no rush. There's nowhere else you need to be right now.

Close your eyes if that feels right. Or soften your gaze toward the floor.

Now, take a deep breath in through your nose... and let it out slowly through your mouth.

Good.

Let's do that again. Breathe in deeply... and release.

One more time. In... and out.

Notice how your body feels. Perhaps your shoulders have dropped a little. Maybe your jaw has unclenched. That's your body's way of saying, "Thank you. I needed this."

The morning breath is about setting an intention for your day. Not a rigid plan, but a gentle direction. A reminder that you have a choice in how you move through the world.

As you continue to breathe, I want you to ask yourself: "How do I want to feel today?"

Not what you need to do. Not what others expect. But how you want to feel.

Maybe it's calm. Maybe it's energized. Maybe it's simply present.

Whatever comes to mind, hold that word gently in your heart.

Now, as you breathe in, imagine you're drawing in that feeling. Breathe in calm. Breathe in energy. Breathe in presence.

And as you breathe out, release anything that doesn't serve that intention. Breathe out tension. Breathe out worry. Breathe out the need to be perfect.

In... and out.

In... and out.

You might notice your mind starting to wander. That's completely normal. You might start thinking about your to-do list, or that conversation you had yesterday, or what you're going to eat for breakfast.

When that happens—and it will—just notice it. Don't judge it. Don't try to force it away.

Simply acknowledge the thought, and then gently bring your attention back to your breath.

In... and out.

This is the practice. Not perfection, but presence. Not control, but compassion.

And here's something important: You might start to feel a little uncomfortable. Maybe your nose itches. Maybe your leg feels restless. Maybe you want to check your phone.

That's okay. That's your mind testing you, seeing if you're really committed to this moment.

If you need to scratch, scratch. If you need to shift, shift. But do it mindfully. Notice the sensation. Acknowledge it. And then return to your breath.

The goal isn't to sit perfectly still like a statue. The goal is to be with yourself, exactly as you are, in this moment.

In... and out.

As we come to the end of this practice, I want you to take one final, deep breath. Fill your lungs completely. Hold it for just a moment... and then let it go with a sigh.

Ahhh.

Beautiful.

When you're ready, gently open your eyes. Take a moment to notice how you feel. Maybe there's a sense of calm. Maybe there's clarity. Maybe there's just a little more space between you and the chaos of the day.

Whatever you feel, it's enough. You are enough.

This morning breath is yours. You can return to it anytime—before a meeting, during a stressful moment, or simply when you need to remember that you have the power to pause.

Thank you for being here. Thank you for choosing yourself.

This is just the beginning. And you're already exactly where you need to be.

Take a deep breath in... and step into your day with intention.

Welcome to your pusterom. Welcome to Drift.
